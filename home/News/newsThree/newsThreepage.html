NSFAS has cleared more than 660,000 students for funding, offering financial stability to those who depend on it to remain in higher education. Yet behind that number is a harder reality: nearly 190,000 students were excluded for not meeting academic progression requirements.


For some, this decision reflects accountability and performance standards; for others, it raises questions about support, pressure and the challenges students face beyond the classroom. As the new academic year approaches, the divide underscores an ongoing conversation about funding, fairness and what success in higher education truly looks like.
